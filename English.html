Ila Sachani

Ila Sachani is an inspiring figure from Moti Vavdi village in Bhavnagar, Gujarat. Born with a condition that left her hands unusable, she learned to embroider using her feet under the guidance of her mother and grandmother, both skilled in Kathiawar embroidery.

Motivators

Ila Sachani's well-wishers include her family, especially her mother and grandmother, who played a crucial role in teaching her embroidery techniques. Her community in Moti Vavdi village and the broader region of Bhavnagar, Gujarat, have also supported her journey .

Ila Sachani's journey is a powerful example of overcoming obstacles with determination and creativity. Here's how she dealt with challenges:

1.Guidance from Loved Ones: Her mother and grandmother played a crucial role in teaching her embroidery techniques, providing both emotional and practical support.

2.Learning to Use Feet: Ila adapted to using her feet for embroidery, developing innovative methods to perform tasks typically done with hands.

3.Persistence: She practiced diligently, refining her skills over time despite the physical challenges with a positive mindset.

4.Resilience: Ila faced her challenges with a strong will and determination, never giving up on her passion.

5.Optimism: Maintaining a positive outlook helped her stay motivated and focused on her goals.

Skill development

Ila Sachani developed her remarkable embroidery skills through dedication, practice, and the guidance of her family. Here's how she honed her craft:

1.Mother and Grandmother's Influence: Both were skilled in Kathiawar embroideryand taught lla the techniques using her feet due to her physical condition.

2.Learning the Basics: Under their guidance, Ila learned the foundational stitches and patterns, which she practiced diligently.

3.Daily Practice: Ila committed to regular practice, refining her skills with each attempt.

4.Experimentation: She experimented with different styles and designs, gradually mastering various embroidery techniques.

5.Innovative Techniques: Ila developed her unique style by combining traditional patterns with her creative ideas.

Ila Sachani excels in Kathiawar embroidery, a traditional Indian embroidery style known for its vibrant colors and intricate patterns. Here are some key techniques she uses:

1.Chain Stitch: Used to create outlines and borders.

2.Buttonhole Stitch: Adds decorative edges and intricate patterns.

3.Cross Stitch: Forms geometric patterns and fills spaces.

4.Mirror Work: Incorporates small mirrors into the design for added sparkle.

5.Kutch Work: Involves creating complex geometric patterns with interwoven stitches.

6.Embossed Patterns: Uses thick threads to create raised designs for a 3D effect.

7.Beadwork: Adds beads for texture and visual interest.
